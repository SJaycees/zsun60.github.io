Optimation Decision Making

  • Total LEARNING OBJECTIVES:

    1. Define decision model and describe the importance of such models.

    2. Understand the two types of decision models: deterministic and probabilistic models.

    3. Understand the steps involved in developing decision models in practical situations.

    4. Understand the use of spreadsheets in developing decision models.

    5. Understand the basic assumptions and properties of linear programming (LP).

    6. Use graphical procedures to solve LP problems with only two variables to understand how LP problems are solved.

    7. Understand special situations such as redundancy, infeasibility, unboundedness, and alternate optimal solutions in LP problems.

Week1

What is decision modeling

  • Scientific approach to decision making
  • Often mathematical
  • Model real-world scenarios
  • Provide insights into solutions

Type of Models

  • Deterministic: All relevant data known with certainty

  • Probabilistic: Some data uncertain, need to use probabilities and statistical knowledge

    • Mental (arranging furniture)
    • Visual (blueprints, road maps)
    • Physical/Scale (aerodynamics, buildings)
    • Mathematical (what we’ll be studying)

  • Spreadsheets in Decision Modelling

    • Computers are an integral part of decision modelling
    • Spreadsheets often replace specialised software
    • Excel built-in functions and add-ins (e.g. Open Solver)
